Who am I?

Hey! How nice of you to pop in.
I’m Christina Metcalfe, a fourth year MSc student reading for a degree in Interaction technology and Design at Umeå University. I was born in Uppsala, Sweden in 1995 and moved to Umeå to study in 2016. Growing up in Uppsala I spent a lot of time singing, I attended Uppsala Musikklasser between 2006 - 2011 and sang in several different choirs. Singing is still something I like doing. I studied technical sciences in high school and during that time I was the student ambassador for Teknikcollege and helped with the work to certify Fyrisskolan.

After graduation I spent the autumn doing a Tekniksprånget internship at SKB (Swedish Nuclear Fuel and Waste Management Co). This is when I decided I wanted to continue studying engineering or technical science to be able to work with developing and making the flow of information between the humans and computer easier. But I wasn’t ready to start studying full time quite yet, I spent three months in Visby on Gotland reading a course in project management and leadership where I also volunteered part time as a shop assistant in a Red Cross shop.

When I moved back to Uppsala I started working at Gammadata Instrument AB as inside sales for the laser department. I sold big laser systems to industry and universities, travelled several times to Germany to attend courses, sales meetings and conferences.
During this year I also volunteered as a youth leader in a church in Knivsta, just north of Stockholm. I helped out during the weekly evening activities and attended camps around Sweden, England and Italy.

In 2016 I decided to move to Umeå and start my MSc degree. I spent my time in Umeå by getting involved in various projects and activities. I joined Umeå student choir and competed in a choir competition in Barcelona. I participated in the buddy program as a buddy leader, working with getting international students involved in the student life at the university. As the project leader, I planned and arranged the DataTjej Conference in February 2019 together with 12 other students.
In my fourth year I travelled to Birmingham, England for an exchange semester at Birmingham City University (BCU). When I came back home I started working for the university as a kind of student ambassador in parallel to my studies. My assignment was to look at how my degree and the courses it contains can develop and ensure a high quality to keep up with the fast changing IT-industry that the students will face once they leave university.

During the summer of 2020 I will be working at Bolagsverket developing the user experience on a new function on their webpage.


In a year’s time, I’ll be finishing my thesis and heading out into industry. I’ll see you then!

Project manager for the DataTjej Conference 2019

DataTjej is a non-profit organization working to promote women and non-binary people of all ages who are interested in, studying or working with IT. DataTjej is probably best known for the annual DataTjej Conference, where companies and students get the opportunity to network. In 2019 the conferance was held in Umeå and I was the project manager and together with a team om 12 women we planned and delivered a three day long conference.
For more information on what DataTjej do, visit their website DataTjej.se

Umeå University

This is where I have spent almost 4 years learning how humans interact with computers and robots, how AI is the future, build full stack systems, construct efficient databases, learnt every word of the mean value theorem and also forgotten most of it, studied for exams, held presentations just like everyone else in my class.

But I have also done things they haven’t. I spent a semester as a buddy leader in the Buddy Program, taking care of international students who arrive in Umeå. We spent at least two night a week playing games, cooking, exercising together to help them adapt to life in Umeå and creating a place where they could feel like they belonged.

In my fourth year I worked for my department at the university, evaluating my program and the courses it contains. To make sure the education we offer the students has a high quality and is up to date with the fast evolving tech-industry.
